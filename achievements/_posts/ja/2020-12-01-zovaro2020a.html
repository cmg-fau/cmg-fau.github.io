Using optical integral field spectroscopy, we present an analysis of the ISM conditions in Minkowski's object (z = 0.0189), a peculiar star-forming dwarf galaxy located in the path of a radio jet from the galaxy NGC 541. Full spectral fitting with PPXF indicates that Minkowski's object primarily consists of a young stellar population $∼ \! 10\, m̊ Myr$ old, confirming that the bulk of the object's stellar mass formed during a recent jet interaction. Minkowski's object exhibits line ratios largely consistent with star formation, although there is evidence for a low level ( $łesssim \! 15 \, \ ̊per \, cent$ ) of contamination from a non-stellar ionizing source.
