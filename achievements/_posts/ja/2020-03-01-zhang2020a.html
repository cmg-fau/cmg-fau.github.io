--- lang: ja ENTRYTYPE: article ID: 2020ApJ...891..177Z abstract: 'We report the Subaru Hyper Suprime-Cam (HSC) discovery of two Ly\ensuremathα blobs (LABs), dubbed z70-1 and z49-1 at z = 6.965 and z = 4.888, respectively, that are Ly\ensuremathα emitters with a bright (log\L\$_Ly\ensuremathα$/[erg s$^-1$]$\gt$ 43.4) and spatially extended Ly\ensuremathα emission, and present the photometric and spectroscopic properties of a total of seven LABs: the two new LABs and five previously known LABs at z = 5.7-6.6. The z70-1 LAB shows extended Ly\ensuremathα emission with a scale length of 1.4 \ensuremath± 0.2 kpc, about three times larger than the UV continuum emission, making z70-1 the most distant LAB identified to date. All of the seven LABs, except z49-1, exhibit no active galactic nucleus (AGN) signatures such as X-ray emission, N V \ensuremathłambda1240 emission, or Ly\ensuremathα line broadening, while z49-1 has a strong C IV \ensuremathłambda1548 emission line indicating an AGN on the basis of the UV-line ratio diagnostics. We carefully model the point-spread functions of the HSC images and conduct two- component exponential profile fitting to the extended Ly\ensuremathα emission of the LABs. The Ly\ensuremathα scale lengths of the core (star-forming region) and halo components are r$_c$ = 0.6-1.2 kpc and r$_h$ = 2.0-13.8 kpc, respectively. The relations between the scale lengths and galaxy properties (Ly\ensuremathα luminosity L$_Ly\ensuremathα$, Ly\ensuremathα rest-frame equivalent width EW$_0$, and UV continuum magnitude M$_UV$) of our LABs are similar to those of Ly\ensuremathα halos (LAHs) identified around star-forming galaxies found previously by the Very Large Telescope/MUSE at similar redshifts, suggesting that our LABs are likely the bright version of high-z LAHs.' adsnote: Provided by the SAO/NASA Astrophysics Data System adsurl: https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020ApJ...891..177Z archiveprefix: arXiv author: - Zhang, Haibin - Ouchi, Masami - Itoh, Ryohei - Shibuya, Takatoshi - Ono, Yoshiaki - Harikane, Yuichi - Inoue, Akio K. - Rauch, Michael - Kikuchihara, Shotaro - Nakajima, Kimihiko - Yajima, Hidenobu - Arata, Shohei - Abe, Makito - Iwata, Ikuru - Kashikawa, Nobunari - Kawanomoto, Satoshi - Kikuta, Satoshi - Kobayashi, Masakazu A.~R. - Kusakabe, Haruka - Mawatari, Ken - Nagao, Tohru - Shimasaku, Kazuhiro - Taniguchi, Yoshiaki doi: 10.3847/1538-4357/ab7917 eid: '177' eprint: '1905.09841' img: - zhang2020a-00.png - zhang2020a-01.png - zhang2020a-02.png - zhang2020a-03.png - zhang2020a-04.png - zhang2020a-05.png - zhang2020a-06.png - zhang2020a-07.png - zhang2020a-08.png img_thumb: zhang2020a-00-thumb.png journal: ID: The Astrophysical Journal name: The Astrophysical Journal keywords: 595, 594, 734, 1146, Astrophysics - Astrophysics of Galaxies link: - anchor: doi url: http://dx.doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/ab7917 month: March number: '2' pages: '177' primaryclass: astro-ph.GA title: CHORUS.
